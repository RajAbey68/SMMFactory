## Part 1: The Hormozi Framework

### 1.1 The Value Equation

Every offer we create must be evaluated against this equation:

```
                Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood
  VALUE = ─────────────────────────────────────────────
                  Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice
```

**Maximize the top. Minimize the bottom.**

| Variable | Definition | How to Increase Value |
|---|---|---|
| **Dream Outcome** | The transformation the customer wants | Paint the vivid after-state, not features |
| **Perceived Likelihood** | How confident they are it will work | Testimonials, case studies, guarantees, proof |
| **Time Delay** ↓ | How long until they get the result | Show speed — "results in 48h", instant access |
| **Effort & Sacrifice** ↓ | What they must give up or do | Make it effortless — done-for-you, turnkey, simple |

> **Rule:** If your offer doesn't make people feel _stupid saying no_, it's not ready.

### 1.2 The Grand Slam Offer

A Grand Slam Offer has five characteristics — **check every campaign against these:**

| # | Element | Test Question |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Unmatchable value** | Is the perceived value 10x the price? |
| 2 | **Premium pricing** | Are we charging enough to deliver exceptionally? |
| 3 | **Risk reversal** | Is there a guarantee that removes all buyer risk? |
| 4 | **Transformation focus** | Are we selling the outcome, not the product? |
| 5 | **Category of one** | Is this offer incomparable to competitors? |

#### Building a Grand Slam Offer (step by step)

1. **Identify the dream outcome** — What does the customer _really_ want?
2. **List every problem** between them and that outcome
3. **Create a solution** for each problem
4. **Trim & stack** — Keep only high-value, low-cost-to-deliver solutions
5. **Name it** — Give the offer a unique, compelling name
6. **Add bonuses** — Remove remaining objections with strategic add-ons
7. **Add urgency/scarcity** — Real deadlines, limited capacity, seasonal windows
8. **Add a guarantee** — Reverse all risk onto yourself

### 1.3 The Core Four (Lead Generation)

Every campaign must map its channels to Hormozi's Core Four:

```
                    People Who KNOW You          People Who DON'T
                  ┌──────────────────────┬──────────────────────┐
  One-to-One      │  1. WARM OUTREACH    │  3. COLD OUTREACH    │
                  │  (DMs, WhatsApp,     │  (personalised       │
                  │   past guests,       │   emails, LinkedIn   │
                  │   referrals)         │   messages)          │
                  ├──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┤
  One-to-Many     │  2. CONTENT          │  4. PAID ADS         │
                  │  (organic posts,     │  (Meta, Google,      │
                  │   stories, email     │   YouTube ads)       │
                  │   newsletters)       │                      │
                  └──────────────────────┴──────────────────────┘
```

> **Rule:** Start with Warm Outreach (cheapest, highest trust). Layer on the rest.  
> **Rule:** Never run paid ads before you've exhausted warm outreach first.  
> **Rule:** "More, Better, New" — do more of what works, make it better, then add new channels.

## Part 2: The Priestley Framework

### 2.1 Key Person of Influence (The 5 Ps)

For personal brand campaigns (like `AICar`), the 5 Ps are the playbook:

| Step | Action | SMMFactory Deliverable |
|---|---|---|
| 1. **Pitch** | Craft a compelling message that opens doors | `Campaign_Summary.md` → Core Message section |
| 2. **Publish** | Share expertise through content | LinkedIn posts, articles, thought leadership |
| 3. **Product** | Turn expertise into scalable offerings | Scorecards, workshops, advisory packages |
| 4. **Profile** | Build visible authority | LinkedIn profile, speaking, podcast appearances |
| 5. **Partnership** | Collaborate to expand reach | Strategic alliances, co-creation, expert networks |

> **Rule:** You must complete Pitch before Publish. Publish before Product. Order matters.

### 2.2 Oversubscribed Method

The goal is to create **more demand than you can serve**. This applies to every campaign:

#### The Signal-to-Capacity Formula

```
  Signals (interested leads)  ≫  Capacity (what you can serve)

  Target ratio: 50 signals per 1 available slot
```

#### Campaign-Driven Enterprise (3 Campaign Types)

| Campaign Type | Cadence | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Perfect Repeatable Week** | Weekly | Consistent, predictable lead generation |
| **Quarterly Spotlight** | Every 90 days | Positioning as authority, events, collaborations |
| **7-11-4 Campaign** | Ongoing | Build engagement around a big idea (not the product) |

#### The 7-11-4 Rule

Before someone buys, they need:
- **7 hours** of content consumption
- **11 touchpoints** with your brand
- **4 different platforms/locations**

> **Rule:** Don't expect conversion from a single ad. Build the 7-11-4 journey.

### 2.3 Scorecard Strategy (Lead Magnet + Qualification)

Instead of giving away a generic PDF, use a **scorecard** that:

1. **Identifies tension** — Gap between customer's current reality and desired state
2. **Provides personalised insight** — "You scored 4/10 on X, here's what that means"
3. **Creates a natural next step** — "Based on your score, here's the path of least resistance"
4. **Qualifies the lead** — You now know their pain points before the first conversation

> **Action:** Every paid_media campaign should have a scorecard or assessment as part of the funnel.

## Part 3: The Tobias Allen Fundamentals (Inside the Hormozi Machine)

> **Source:** Tobias Allen — Director of Marketing, acquisition.com. 10+ years in direct response marketing. Built a 29,000-person affiliate programme, orchestrated the $100M Leads launch (500,000+ registrants), driven millions in sales.

### 3.1 Dramatic Demonstration ("Elevators & Cake")

**The principle:** You must GET ATTENTION before anything else matters. If you lose them before you start, nothing downstream works.

**How it works:**
- **Otis Elevators** couldn't sell elevators because people feared they'd fall. They hired P.T. Barnum, who had the founder stand on an elevator and cut the cable with a saber at the World Trade Fair. The elevator halted safely. Sales skyrocketed. They're now in every building in the world.
- **Claude Hopkins** had to sell Suet (boring cake ingredient). He made the world's largest cake, took it on a road show town-to-town, drew crowds, and built a distribution empire.
- **Alex Hormozi's books** are meta-demonstrations: $100M Offers (a book about irresistible offers, listed for $0.99 — itself an irresistible offer). $100M Leads (used every lead method in the book to register 500,000 people for the launch event).

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Every campaign needs a "dramatic demonstration" moment. What is the spectacle?  
> **For KLEst:** Before/after transformation video of the villa experience. Time-lapse of sunset from the lake deck.  
> **For AICar:** A live case study or public "AI readiness audit" that demonstrates the methodology.

### 3.2 The MAN Formula (Target the Right Person)

**Money — Authority — Need**

| Element | Question | Anti-pattern |
|---|---|---|
| **Money** | Can they afford what you're selling? | Selling expensive courses to students |
| **Authority** | Are they the decision maker? | Pitching to the wrong person in the org |
| **Need** | Do they have a problem you solve? | Advertising to people who don't need you |

**Key insight from Tobias:** Don't sell boilers to homeowners — sell to plumbers. The plumber is the one with authority who recommends the brand when homeowner Sally asks "who do you recommend?" Find the person who ACTUALLY makes the buying decision.

**Going upmarket:** There's more money to be made when you increase sophistication of your target. Go after avatars with more money, not less.

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Before every campaign, run the MAN check. Who has the money, authority, and need?  
> **For KLEst:** Families/groups with budget for private villa (not budget backpackers). The person booking = usually the parent/organiser.  
> **For AICar:** Tier 1 & 2 AI companies. The hiring decision maker is the VP of Customer Success or Head of AI Solutions, not HR.

### 3.3 The Bridge (Offer Packaging)

Every sale takes someone from **Point A** (where they are) to **Point B** (where they want to be). The bridge is your product/offer. You can package that journey differently:

| Vehicle | What it means | Price |
|---|---|---|
| 🏎️ **Sports car** | Fast, premium, compressed time | $$$$ |
| 🐎 **Horse** | Slower, more experiential | $$ |
| 📖 **Book** | Self-service, DIY | $ |
| 🎉 **Party bus** | Fun, social, unexpected | $$$ |

**Rory Sutherland's Eurostar analogy:** They spent billions to shave minutes off the journey. If they'd spent that money on champagne and models, people wouldn't want to get off.

**Perception is reality:** You can spin any constraint into a strength. Small agency? "Your account gets dedicated attention, not a junior intern." Only 3 people? "That's why our quality is unmatched."

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Package multiple bridge options in the offer. Don't just offer one journey.  
> **For KLEst:** Sports car = full villa buyout with concierge. Horse = room-only at a fair rate. Book = self-service Airbnb listing.  
> **For AICar:** Sports car = 2-day intensive on-site ($50K). Horse = 6-month advisory ($5K/mo). Book = public content (free → credibility builder).

### 3.4 Going for the Bullseye (The REAL Reason People Buy)

**Don't sell what sounds good. Sell the REAL reason.**

| Product | Obvious target | Real target | Real motivation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Viagra | Single men wanting performance | Couples | Intimacy |
| Folgers Coffee | Coffee drinkers wanting taste | Everyone | The SMELL — childhood memory of mom's kitchen |
| PT Cruiser | Practical car buyers | Subconscious rebels | "Scary gangster" — power on the road |
| Nespresso | Coffee machine users | Status-seekers | George Clooney = sophistication brand |

**Clotaire Rapaille's reptilian brain method:** Lock people in a room, ask why they buy. They give the "reason that sounds good." Let them nap. Ask again when they wake — the answers come from the subconscious. THOSE are the real reasons.

**Cialis vs Viagra:** Cialis spent 6 months on research and found the real buyer was women. They led with intimacy instead of performance. Stole massive market share.

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Run the "reason that sounds good vs real reason" test on every campaign.  
> **For KLEst:** Sounds good = "nice villa." Real reason = "I want my family together in one place, no logistics, no stress, someone else handles everything."  
> **For AICar:** Sounds good = "I'm looking for a CS role." Real reason = "I want to be the expert people come to. I want to matter."

### 3.5 Salesmanship in Print (Ogilvy's "We Sell or Else")

**Your funnel must actually SELL. Not just look pretty.**

Most people's marketing is "impotent" — it doesn't close. Common mistake: create organic content that gets views on TikTok → run it as a paid ad → it doesn't convert → "advertising doesn't work for me."

**The fix:**
1. **Build the complete sales presentation first** — a full message that can close the sale on its own
2. **Then pull pieces from it for ads** — each ad is a fragment of your core sales message
3. **Keep congruence** — the promise in the ad must match the promise on the landing page
4. **Ads should be ads** — they need benefits, objection-handling, proof, and a CTA. Entertainment ≠ salesmanship.

**Think of each ad as a salesperson.** Would you hire a salesperson who just tells jokes and never asks for the sale?

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Write the core sales message BEFORE creating ads. Every ad must contain at least one: benefit, proof element, or objection-handler.  
> **For KLEst:** The landing page IS the sales presentation. Ads pull specific angles from it (family togetherness, lake views, free airport pickup).  
> **For AICar:** LinkedIn posts must contain proof + CTA, not just thought leadership air.

### 3.6 The Elephant in a Dark Room (Kill Vanity Metrics)

**Looking at metrics in isolation gives you a distorted picture.**

Person holding the trunk thinks it's a snake. Person holding the leg thinks it's a tree. Person on the back thinks it's a horse. **Turn on the light.**

| Vanity Metric | Reality Check |
|---|---|
| "60% close rate" | That's on warm referrals. Cold traffic = 20-30% — and that's NORMAL |
| "8x ROAS!" | Could quadruple spend and make 4x the money at 3x ROAS |
| "50% of revenue from email" | You're bottom-heavy. Neglecting top-of-funnel. 20% of 1,000 > 50% of 100 |
| "Low opt-in rate" | Maybe it's qualifying well — check downstream close rate |
| "600 views only" | One of those views was someone with a Netflix show. Quality > quantity |

**The key question: "How can we spend MORE money on ads profitably?"** Not "how do we get the best ROAS." Scale beats efficiency if the math works.

**Alex's content pivot:** Went from broad viral content (protein stacks, gym routines) to deep business education. Views dropped. But the RIGHT people started watching. Quality of leads > quantity of views.

> **SMMFactory Rule:** Always tie metrics back to the business outcome (revenue, bookings, conversations). Never optimise for a single metric in isolation.  
> **For KLEst:** Track bookings and revenue, not just ad impressions. A $2 CPM with zero bookings = waste.  
> **For AICar:** Track conversations and interviews, not just LinkedIn impressions.

### 3.7 Operational Values (What Makes Hormozi's Machine Win)

These are the behavioural principles that compound over time:

| Value | Meaning | Applied to SMMFactory |
|---|---|---|
| **Volume negates luck** | More reps = more data = more results. The person who does 100 things while others do 5 will always win. | Ship more campaigns. Test more angles. Send more messages. |
| **Success likes speed** | How can we do this by tomorrow? Compress time windows. Parkinson's law: work expands to fill the time given. | Every campaign phase has a hard deadline. No drift. |
| **Competitive greatness** | Do more than is required. Weekends, late nights, extra effort. Complacency kills. | Don't settle for "good enough" creative. Push for excellent. |
| **Sincere candor** | Hard conversations grow businesses. If something is wrong, say it. | If a campaign isn't working, flag it immediately. Don't protect egos. |
| **Unimpeachable character** | Be someone others are proud to associate with. | Brand integrity matters. No misleading claims, no bait-and-switch. |

> **The Ogilvy Method (from Tobias):** Find the person who has the results you want. Study them obsessively for 2 years. Put your shoulder against the wheel. Stop messing about. Get serious. Get good.

## Rules for All Campaigns (Non-Negotiable)

### Strategy Rules (Hormozi + Priestley)
1. **No campaign launches without a Grand Slam Offer check** — use the value equation
2. **Warm outreach ALWAYS comes before paid ads** — cheaper, higher trust, faster feedback
3. **Every campaign needs a scorecard or signal-collection mechanism** — never advertise to nowhere
4. **Build the 7-11-4 journey** — one ad is not a campaign
5. **Price for premium** — never compete on low price
6. **Add a guarantee** — remove all buyer risk
7. **"More, Better, New"** — optimise what works before adding new channels
8. **Collect signals, not sales** — let demand build before converting
9. **Delight existing customers** — they are your best marketing channel
10. **Document everything** — every campaign ends with a retrospective that feeds the next one

### Execution Rules (Tobias Allen)
11. **Every campaign needs a dramatic demonstration** — what is the spectacle that gets attention?
12. **Run the MAN check before spending money** — Money, Authority, Need. Are you targeting the decision maker?
13. **Offer multiple bridges** — sports car, horse, and book. Don't force one-size-fits-all.
14. **Find the REAL reason** — not the reason that sounds good, but the subconscious driver
15. **Sales presentation BEFORE ads** — write the core message. Ads are fragments of it. Keep congruence.
16. **Turn on the light** — never optimise a single metric in isolation. Tie everything to the business outcome.
17. **Volume negates luck** — more reps, more data, more results. Speed compounds.
18. **Sincere candor** — if it's not working, say so immediately. Hard conversations grow businesses.
